Greetings!

I am Gyuwon Jung, a Ph.D. student in the School of Computing at Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (KAIST). I am an active member of the Interactive Computing Laboratory, advised by Professor Uichin Lee. I earned my M.S. degree in Knowledge Service Engineering from KAIST in 2018 and my B.S. in Chemistry from KAIST in 2016.

As a researcher in the field of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), I am highly interested in supporting users in understanding themselves through data collected in their everyday lives. My recent research primarily focuses on Investigating Causal Relationships Between Contexts and Human Behaviors by Analyzing Mobile Data (i.e., data collected from mobile devices such as smartphones and wearables). I believe that such insights could contribute to enhancing the lives of individuals who need to track and manage themselves in various health scenarios.

My research interests can be summarized with the following keywords:

Mobile Data, Causal Inference, Counterfactual Explanations, Personal Informatics, Human Behavior Change, Digital Health, Digital Therapeutics, and User Engagement
